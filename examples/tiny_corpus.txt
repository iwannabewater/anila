Anila is a small language model training project.
It learns from plain text and predicts the next token.
Good engineering makes experiments easier to repeat.
Small models are useful because every part can be inspected.
The tokenizer turns text into ids, and the model turns ids into logits.
Training is a loop of batches, loss, gradients, optimizer steps, and checkpoints.
Anila favors simple interfaces and clear implementation.
From zero to one means the first version should actually run.
